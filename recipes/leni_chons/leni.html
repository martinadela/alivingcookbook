The German words Laib and Leib are pronounced the same but have different meanings.
Laib = loaf
Leib = body
I – Leibhaftigkeit = physicality
II – Verleiblichung = incarnation
III – Leibesfrucht = fruit of the body, fetus
IV – Einverleibung = incorporation
Eigenschaft = quality, characteristic
Leibeigenschaft = serfdom, servitude, slavery.
Laibeigenschaft = characteristic of the loaf.

What happens if the loaf (Laib) becomes a body (Leib) whose ability to act is limited to its life-sustaining breath? Even that breath quickly transforms into a mere reaction to its external circumstances, becomes hectic and abrupt. How much freedom do we allow our own bodies? How much are they subject to unquestioned social beliefs and conventions we declare as our own? Where does the Inside end, where does the Outside begin?
